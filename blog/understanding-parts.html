Understanding Your Parts: An Introduction to IFS

November 20, 2024

Have you ever noticed different voices in your head? The part that wants to take risks and the part that holds you back? The part that's confident and the part that's filled with doubt? In Internal Family Systems therapy, we understand these as "parts" - distinct aspects of your psyche, each with their own perspective, feelings, and intentions.

What Are Parts?

Unlike other therapeutic approaches that might view these inner voices as problems to be solved or symptoms to be eliminated, IFS recognizes them as valuable members of your internal system. Each part, even the ones that seem problematic or painful, is trying to help you in some way.

Think of your psyche as an internal family. Just like in a family, each member (or part) has their own personality, their own concerns, and their own way of trying to keep you safe or help you thrive. Some parts might be young and vulnerable, carrying painful memories from the past. Others might be protective, working tirelessly to make sure you never feel that pain again.

Common Types of Parts

In IFS, we often encounter several types of parts:

  • Managers - These parts try to keep you in control and prevent painful feelings from emerging. They might show up as the inner critic, the perfectionist, or the people-pleaser.
  • Firefighters - When painful feelings do emerge, these parts react quickly to extinguish them. They might use distraction, substances, or other impulsive behaviors.
  • Exiles - These are often younger parts carrying painful emotions and memories that the other parts are trying to protect you from.

Why This Matters

When we begin to see our internal experience through the lens of parts, something profound shifts. Instead of being at war with ourselves - trying to eliminate the anxious part, suppress the angry part, or override the fearful part - we can begin to get curious about them.

What if that anxious part is actually trying to keep you safe? What if the angry part is protecting a younger, more vulnerable part that was hurt in the past? What if the part that procrastinates is actually afraid of failure or criticism?

The Journey Inward

IFS therapy invites us on a journey of getting to know these parts - not to fix them or make them go away, but to understand them, appreciate their positive intentions, and help them release the burdens they've been carrying.

This is where the magic happens. When parts feel truly seen and understood, when they trust that there's a compassionate presence (what we call Self) that can handle what they've been protecting you from, they naturally begin to relax and transform.

Beginning Your Own Exploration

If you're curious about exploring your own parts, here's a simple practice to begin:

  1. Notice when you're feeling a strong emotion or having a strong reaction to something
  2. Instead of identifying with it ("I am anxious"), try seeing it as a part ("A part of me feels anxious")
  3. Get curious about this part. What is it concerned about? What is it trying to protect you from?
  4. See if you can find some appreciation for its efforts, even if its methods aren't always helpful

Remember, this is just the beginning. Working with parts in therapy provides a safe, supported space to go deeper into this exploration. If you're interested in experiencing IFS therapy, I'd be honored to guide you on this journey.
