The Power of Self-Leadership in Healing

November 10, 2024

At the heart of Internal Family Systems therapy lies a profound truth: beneath all our protective parts, our wounded parts, and our reactive patterns, there exists a core Self that is naturally calm, curious, compassionate, and clear. This Self is not something we need to create or develop - it's already there, waiting to be accessed.

What is Self?

In IFS, we use the term "Self" (with a capital S) to describe this core essence of who we are. It's the part of us that can observe our thoughts and feelings without being overwhelmed by them. It's the wise, compassionate presence that can hold space for all our parts, even the difficult ones.

You've likely experienced moments of being in Self, even if you didn't have a name for it. Think of a time when you felt deeply connected to yourself and others, when you could see a situation clearly without being caught up in reactivity, or when you felt a natural compassion for someone who was struggling. These are glimpses of Self.

The Qualities of Self

Dr. Richard Schwartz, the founder of IFS, describes Self through what he calls the "8 C's":

  • Calm - A sense of physiological relaxation and peace
  • Clarity - Ability to perceive situations accurately
  • Curiosity - Genuine interest in understanding ourselves and others
  • Compassion - Warmth and care for ourselves and others
  • Confidence - Trust in our ability to handle what comes
  • Courage - Willingness to face difficult truths and feelings
  • Creativity - Ability to find new solutions and perspectives
  • Connectedness - Sense of being part of something larger

When Parts Step Aside

The challenge is that our parts often don't trust Self to handle things. They've been working so hard to protect us, manage our lives, and keep painful feelings at bay. The idea of stepping aside and letting Self lead can feel terrifying to them.

This is where the therapeutic process becomes so important. In IFS therapy, we don't try to force parts to step aside. Instead, we build a relationship with them. We get curious about their concerns. We appreciate their efforts. We help them see that Self is actually capable of handling what they've been protecting us from.

The Transformation of Self-Leadership

When parts begin to trust Self, something remarkable happens. The anxious part that's been vigilantly scanning for danger can finally relax. The angry part that's been protecting a wounded child can soften. The perfectionist that's been driving us relentlessly can take a break.

This doesn't mean these parts disappear. Rather, they transform. They can take on new, more life-giving roles. The protector becomes a wise advisor. The wounded child finds healing. The perfectionist becomes a healthy striving for excellence.

Accessing Self in Daily Life

While deep healing work often requires the support of therapy, there are ways to begin accessing Self in your daily life:

  1. Notice when you're blended - When you're completely identified with a part's perspective, you're "blended." Simply noticing this is the first step.
  2. Take a breath - Physical space can help create psychological space. A few deep breaths can help parts step back slightly.
  3. Get curious - Ask yourself, "What part of me is feeling this way?" This simple question can help you access Self's curiosity.
  4. Appreciate your parts - Even when their methods aren't helpful, parts are trying to help. Acknowledging this can help them relax.

The Journey Continues

Developing Self-leadership is not a destination but a journey. It's a practice of repeatedly coming back to this centered, compassionate place within ourselves. Some days it's easier than others. Some situations trigger our parts more strongly than others.

But each time we access Self, even for a moment, we're strengthening this capacity. We're showing our parts that there is indeed a wise, compassionate leader within who can handle life's challenges. We're healing from the inside out.

The journey of Self-leadership is deeply personal and often benefits from the support of a trained therapist. If you're feeling called to explore this work more deeply, I would be honored to walk alongside you on this path.
