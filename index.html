The basic principles of IFS

The mind is a collection of "parts," such as the inner critic or the perfectionist, not a single unified self. These parts are natural aspects of the mind with their own feelings, perspectives, and beliefs.
Everyone has a core "Self" that is inherently calm, confident, clear, and compassionate. The goal of IFS is for this Self to become the leader of the internal system, guiding the other parts.
Even "negative" parts are seen as trying to help, often based on experiences from the past, such as trauma. For example, a part that is extremely critical may be trying to protect the individual from criticism.
The goal of IFS is not to get rid of parts but to heal them. Parts that carry burdens from past experiences can be "unburdened" through the Self's compassion and wisdom.
Life experiences, like trauma, can cause parts to become "stuck" in their roles and carry burdens of extreme emotions and beliefs. Unburdening allows these parts to return to their natural, healthy state.
The model views the mind as naturally multiple and considers all parts to be inherently good, which is a non-judgmental way of looking at mental health.

What to Expect in Therapy

IFS therapy is a collaborative process where you remain in the lead of your own healing. My role is to provide a safe, supportive space and guide you as you explore your inner world.

Sessions typically involve:

  • Developing awareness of different parts of yourself
  • Understanding the protective roles these parts play
  • Accessing your core Self - the calm, compassionate leader within
  • Working with parts to release burdens from past experiences
  • Integrating insights into your daily life

The process is gentle and goes at your own pace. You're always in control of what we explore and when.

Introduction

This Privacy Policy explains how I, Tamsin Taylor (operating as Tamsin Urquhart), collect, use, and protect your personal information as a psychotherapist and data controller.

I am committed to protecting your privacy and complying with data protection laws including the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

1. What Information I Collect

Personal Data Collected:

Contact Information:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number (if provided)
  • Information submitted through the contact form

Therapy Records:

  • Clinical notes from therapy sessions
  • Assessment information
  • Any relevant medical or psychological history you share
  • Details of our therapeutic relationship

Payment Information:

  • Bank details (for BACS transfer)
  • PayPal transaction records
  • Invoice information

2. Legal Basis for Processing

I process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  1. Contract: Processing is necessary to fulfill my therapeutic contract with you
  2. Legitimate Interests: Processing is necessary for providing therapy and maintaining appropriate clinical records
  3. Legal Obligation: Where required by professional bodies, law, or for safeguarding purposes
  4. Consent: For any processing activities where I specifically request your consent

3. How I Use Your Information

I use your personal data to:

  • Respond to enquiries submitted via email, text message, or contact form
  • Conduct therapy sessions and maintain clinical records
  • Process payments for services
  • Comply with professional and legal obligations
  • Manage safeguarding concerns where appropriate

4. How Long I Keep Your Data

Contact Enquiries:

  • If we do not proceed with therapy: Deleted within 6 months of last contact
  • If we proceed with therapy: Records retained as below

Therapy Records:

  • Clinical records are retained for a minimum of 7 years after our last session
  • This is in line with UKCP requirements and professional guidelines
  • After this period, records are securely destroyed

Financial Records:

  • Kept for 7 years for tax and accounting purposes

5. Who Has Access to Your Information

Your information may be shared with:

Third Parties (only when necessary):

  • Your GP (with your consent)
  • Other healthcare professionals involved in your care (with your consent)
  • Insurance companies or EAPs (if applicable, with your consent)
  • Professional supervisor (for clinical supervision - identifiable information minimized)
  • Legal authorities (only if required by law or for safeguarding)

Important: All sharing is done in accordance with professional ethics codes and data protection law. I will always discuss with you before sharing information where possible, except in exceptional circumstances (e.g., safeguarding concerns).

6. How Your Data is Stored

Digital Records:

Paper Records:

7. Your Rights Under GDPR

You have the right to:

  1. Access: Request a copy of the personal data I hold about you
  2. Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate data

  4. Restriction: Request restricted processing of your data
  5. Data Portability: Receive your data in a structured format
  6. Object: Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  7. Withdraw Consent: Where processing is based on consent

Important Notes: Some rights may be limited due to my professional obligations to maintain records. I will respond to requests within 30 days. I may need to verify your identity before processing requests. There is no charge for most requests.
